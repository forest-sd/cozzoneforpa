Navigating County Governance in Pennsylvania

Published: 2024

Understanding county governance is essential for effective civic participation. Pennsylvania's 67 counties each have their own governmental structure that directly impacts residents' daily lives.

County Commissioner System

Most Pennsylvania counties are governed by a three-member Board of Commissioners. Commissioners are elected at-large and serve four-year terms. They are responsible for the county budget, tax rates, and oversight of county departments.

Key County Functions

  • Elections administration and voter registration
  • Property assessment and tax collection
  • Courts and judicial administration
  • Human services and social programs
  • Parks, libraries, and cultural facilities
  • Emergency management and public safety

How to Participate

Attend commissioner meetings, which are open to the public. Review meeting agendas and minutes published online. Submit public comments on proposed policies. Run for office or serve on appointed boards and commissions.

Chester County

Chester County, the wealthiest county in Pennsylvania, faces unique governance challenges including managing growth, preserving open space, maintaining infrastructure, and balancing development with quality of life.
